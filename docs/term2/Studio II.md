## ★ Problematize Yourself

<div style="font-size:12px; line-height:1.5;">

<h2>5 Errors</h2>

<p>
  <strong>1 Being too digital and lacking physical translation</strong><br>
  I relied heavily on screens, code, and systems, postponing the moment where my
  actions became physical prototypes.
</p>

<p>
  <strong>2 Mistaking technical failure for conceptual failure</strong><br>
  When systems broke, models failed, or code didn’t function as intended, I would find
  myself shifting the intentions of the project rather based off my skills to make it work.
</p>

<p>
  <strong>3 Trying to define the actions before letting them respond</strong><br>
  I limited the project’s ability to evolve through iteration, failure, and response.
</p>

<p>
  <strong>4 Breaking from my architectural background</strong><br>
  I struggled to shift from a spatial and technical architectural mindset toward a more
  theoretical and hybrid mode of inquiry, often seeking structure and resolution too early despite
  actively working throughout the semester to unlearn this tendency.
</p>

<p>
  <strong>5 To create a continuous path throughout the semester</strong><br>
  I struggled to create a continuous trajectory throughout the semester, as unresolved gaps and the constant emergence of new
  interests fragmented the project and made it difficult to focus deeply on a single direction.
</p>

<h2>5 Fascinations</h2>

<p>
  <strong>1 Data privacy</strong><br>
  This semester I deep dived into how our personal data is exploited and sold. It
  was scary and interesting.
</p>

<p>
  <strong>2 Digital personas and AI self-extension</strong><br>
  The personas we construct—online identities, behavioral patterns, decision
  histories—fascinate me as precursors to AI personas.
</p>

<p>
  <strong>3 Technology as a design medium, not a tool</strong><br>
  Rather than using technology to execute ideas, I am increasingly treating it as the
  material itself—something that shapes form, behavior, ethics, and authorship from within the
  design process.
</p>

<p>
  <strong>4 Breaking technophobia / The moment control is surrendered</strong><br>
  My personal relationship with technology shifted throughout the semester as a form of
  acceptance and fascination. The most compelling point in my work emerges when I allow
  systems to decide, speak, or reflect something back to me.
</p>

<p>
  <strong>5 What are the real restrictions?</strong>
</p>

</div>
