## ★ The Thesis Position[¶](#thesis-position "Permanent link")

<div style="border:2px solid black; margin:24px 0;">
  <div style="background:#c8d400; padding:8px 16px; font-weight:bold; font-size:12px; letter-spacing:0.08em;">WHAT THE LITERATURE GIVES YOU — AND WHERE IT RUNS OUT</div>
  <div style="display:grid; grid-template-columns:1fr 1fr; border-top:2px solid black;">
    <div style="border-right:2px solid black; padding:16px; border-bottom:2px solid black;">
      <p style="font-size:12px; font-weight:bold; margin:0 0 6px 0;">ZUBOFF — Surveillance Capitalism</p>
      <p style="font-size:13px; margin:0 0 8px 0;">Human experience as free raw material for behavioral data. Frames vulnerability as something imposed on you.</p>
      <p style="font-size:12px; font-style:italic; color:#555; margin:0;">Gap: doesn't occupy deliberate, authored vulnerability as a design position.</p>
    </div>
    <div style="padding:16px; border-bottom:2px solid black;">
      <p style="font-size:12px; font-weight:bold; margin:0 0 6px 0;">HAYLES — How We Became Posthuman</p>
      <p style="font-size:13px; margin:0 0 8px 0;">Identity as a pattern of information expressed through the body, not contained by it. The body is not a limitation — it's the theoretical commitment.</p>
      <p style="font-size:12px; font-style:italic; color:#555; margin:0;">Gap: doesn't extend to the designer's ability to work with that threshold.</p>
    </div>
    <div style="border-right:2px solid black; padding:16px;">
      <p style="font-size:12px; font-weight:bold; margin:0 0 6px 0;">LUPTON — The Quantified Self</p>
      <p style="font-size:13px; margin:0 0 8px 0;">Knowing capitalism: users voluntarily enacting the values of systems extracting from them. Vulnerability framed as risk.</p>
      <p style="font-size:12px; font-style:italic; color:#555; margin:0;">Gap: doesn't name the space between exposure and protection.</p>
    </div>
    <div style="padding:16px;">
      <p style="font-size:12px; font-weight:bold; margin:0 0 6px 0;">SAMPAIO — Data Visualization as Portraiture</p>
      <p style="font-size:13px; margin:0 0 8px 0;">Data visualization as subjective expression, not just quantification. Gestures at the tension between exposure and protection.</p>
      <p style="font-size:12px; font-style:italic; color:#555; margin:0;">Gap: doesn't make that tension a design material.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div style="background:black; color:#c8d400; padding:16px; border-top:2px solid black;">
    <p style="font-size:13px; font-weight:bold; margin:0 0 6px 0;">THE GAP — WHERE THIS WORK LIVES</p>
    <p style="font-size:14px; margin:0; line-height:1.6;">Not total exposure (Poisonous Antidote). Not total protection (privacy by design).<br><strong>Conscious calibration of self-exposure</strong> — the act of deciding what to render, how, and for whom — as a design act. That's where consent becomes something a designer can work with rather than just a legal checkbox.</p>
  </div>
</div>

## ★ The Projects[¶](#the-projects "Permanent link")

<div style="border:2px solid black; margin:16px 0;">
  <div style="background:#e74c3c; color:white; padding:12px 16px; display:grid; grid-template-columns:1fr auto;">
    <div>
      <span style="font-size:11px; letter-spacing:0.1em; opacity:0.8;">TERM 1 · DESIGN DIALOGUES</span>
      <div style="font-size:20px; font-weight:bold; margin-top:4px;">KINETIC VEIN</div>
    </div>
    <div style="text-align:right; font-size:11px; opacity:0.8; align-self:end;">3D print · NeoPixel · screen time data</div>
  </div>
  <div style="padding:16px;">
    <p style="font-size:13px; margin:0 0 10px 0;">A 3D printed Voronoi arm cuff paired with a NeoPixel IV drip installation. Screen time input changes LED state — slow blue breathing at low consumption, frantic red strobe at high. Screen time becomes something that flows through your veins, that the body processes, that has a physiological cost.</p>
    <p style="font-size:12px; font-style:italic; color:#555; margin:0; border-left:3px solid #e74c3c; padding-left:10px;">In the arc: makes bodily data externally legible. It doesn't extract silently — it shows you.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div style="border:2px solid black; margin:16px 0;">
  <div style="background:#8e44ad; color:white; padding:12px 16px; display:grid; grid-template-columns:1fr auto;">
    <div>
      <span style="font-size:11px; letter-spacing:0.1em; opacity:0.8;">TERM 1 · DESIGN STUDIO 1</span>
      <div style="font-size:20px; font-weight:bold; margin-top:4px;">COLLECTIVE HANNAH</div>
    </div>
    <div style="text-align:right; font-size:11px; opacity:0.8; align-self:end;">Ollama · Anti-Gravity · 6 AI personas</div>
  </div>
  <div style="padding:16px;">
    <p style="font-size:13px; margin:0 0 10px 0;">Six distinct AI personas constructed from different slices of personal data — location history, sleep data, biometrics, messages, search history, calendar. Each persona represents a different facet of identity as constructed by a machine from a data fragment. The six personas don't agree. They contradict each other. There is no unified self — only the sum of what different systems have decided you mean.</p>
    <p style="font-size:12px; font-style:italic; color:#555; margin:0; border-left:3px solid #8e44ad; padding-left:10px;">In the arc: the fragmentation project. Identity is not a stable thing the self holds — it's a pattern that can be assembled, multiplied, and contested.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div style="border:2px solid black; margin:16px 0;">
  <div style="background:#2980b9; color:white; padding:12px 16px; display:grid; grid-template-columns:1fr auto;">
    <div>
      <span style="font-size:11px; letter-spacing:0.1em; opacity:0.8;">TERM 1 · DESIGN STUDIO 1</span>
      <div style="font-size:20px; font-weight:bold; margin-top:4px;">MARKET OF ME</div>
    </div>
    <div style="text-align:right; font-size:11px; opacity:0.8; align-self:end;">JavaScript · Supabase · streets of Barcelona</div>
  </div>
  <div style="padding:16px;">
    <p style="font-size:13px; margin:0 0 10px 0;">A speculative biometric data stock market deployed as a public action. Personal biometric data — heartrate, steps, stress proxies — treated as a tradeable commodity with fluctuating values. Deployed on the streets of Barcelona, confronting passersby with what their body data is worth and to whom.</p>
    <p style="font-size:12px; font-style:italic; color:#555; margin:0; border-left:3px solid #2980b9; padding-left:10px;">In the arc: the confrontation project. Takes the abstract logic of surveillance capitalism and makes it literal and participatory.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div style="border:2px solid black; margin:16px 0;">
  <div style="background:#27ae60; color:white; padding:12px 16px; display:grid; grid-template-columns:1fr auto;">
    <div>
      <span style="font-size:11px; letter-spacing:0.1em; opacity:0.8;">TERM 2 · COGNITIVE ORGIES I</span>
      <div style="font-size:20px; font-weight:bold; margin-top:4px;">AURA</div>
    </div>
    <div style="text-align:right; font-size:11px; opacity:0.8; align-self:end;">RPi Pico W · heartbeat sensor · bacterial cellulose</div>
  </div>
  <div style="padding:16px;">
    <p style="font-size:13px; margin:0 0 10px 0;">A wearable, organ-like earpiece that converts heartbeat data into NeoPixel light patterns, encased in bacterial cellulose — a bio-based material grown in the lab. The heartbeat is involuntary, pre-rational, constant. Making it visible is an exposure of something never meant to be read by anyone else. The housing is itself a living material, grown — the biological and the digital in conversation, not opposition.</p>
    <p style="font-size:12px; font-style:italic; color:#555; margin:0; border-left:3px solid #27ae60; padding-left:10px;">In the arc: the most intimate scale. Operates at the level of the body's own signals.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div style="border:2px solid black; margin:16px 0;">
  <div style="background:#d35400; color:white; padding:12px 16px; display:grid; grid-template-columns:1fr auto;">
    <div>
      <span style="font-size:11px; letter-spacing:0.1em; opacity:0.8;">TERM 2 · COGNITIVE ORGIES II</span>
      <div style="font-size:20px; font-weight:bold; margin-top:4px;">ECHO OF MIND</div>
    </div>
    <div style="text-align:right; font-size:11px; opacity:0.8; align-self:end;">RPi 5 · Whisper · GPT agent · FLUX.1 · GitHub archive</div>
  </div>
  <div style="padding:16px;">
    <p style="font-size:13px; margin:0 0 10px 0;">A standalone physical device. You speak → Whisper transcribes → a GPT agent processes → FLUX.1 renders as image → result archived to public GitHub. A persistent visual record of thought — externalized, AI-mediated, domestic, present. The act of speaking to it becomes a kind of authorship. The archive fills over time, connecting strangers through their interior lives.</p>
    <p style="font-size:12px; font-style:italic; color:#555; margin:0; border-left:3px solid #d35400; padding-left:10px;">In the arc: where identity is actively constructed through the pipeline — not just sensed or extracted, but generated.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div style="border:2px solid black; margin:16px 0;">
  <div style="background:#ff6b9d; color:white; padding:12px 16px; display:grid; grid-template-columns:1fr auto;">
    <div>
      <span style="font-size:11px; letter-spacing:0.1em; opacity:0.8;">TERM 2 · COGNITIVE ORGIES III</span>
      <div style="font-size:20px; font-weight:bold; margin-top:4px;">MOCHI</div>
    </div>
    <div style="text-align:right; font-size:11px; opacity:0.8; align-self:end;">ESP32-S3 · OLED · pixel art · open source</div>
  </div>
  <div style="padding:16px;">
    <p style="font-size:13px; margin:0 0 10px 0;">A self-contained cyberdeck companion — no cloud, no app store, no extraction. Feed it data through physical buttons: sleep, water, food. It responds with its body. Tend to it and it grows. Neglect it and it shrinks. Abandon it and it dies. Not a wellness tracker. Not a fitness app. A mirror with a body — a physical object that makes the invisible exchange between you and your data visible, chosen, and consequential.</p>
    <p style="font-size:12px; font-style:italic; color:#555; margin:0; border-left:3px solid #ff6b9d; padding-left:10px;">In the arc: the first act of a trilogy. Here, you choose to tend it. In Find Your Match, it gets extracted. In Safe Hands, it outlives you.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div style="border:2px solid black; margin:16px 0;">
  <div style="background:#c8d400; color:black; padding:12px 16px; display:grid; grid-template-columns:1fr auto;">
    <div>
      <span style="font-size:11px; letter-spacing:0.1em; opacity:0.7;">TERM 3 · MDEF FEST · 18 JUNE 2026</span>
      <div style="font-size:20px; font-weight:bold; margin-top:4px;">FIND YOUR MATCH AT ELISAVA</div>
    </div>
    <div style="text-align:right; font-size:11px; opacity:0.7; align-self:end;">Next.js · Supabase · Tally · Resend · 16 participants</div>
  </div>
  <div style="padding:16px;">
    <p style="font-size:13px; margin:0 0 10px 0;">QR codes. A dating form. 15 scored questions secretly mapped to 7 real institutional data buyers. A live candlestick stock market. 16 participants. Each finds a folder with their anonymous ID and a euro value — the price their data would fetch on the open market. Inside: a receipt itemizing exactly which answers triggered which buyer. And a match card. A real person. Computed by an algorithm that weighted the filler questions — the ones data markets can't monetize — at 3×.</p>
    <p style="font-size:13px; margin:0 0 10px 0; font-weight:bold;">"You came looking for a date. We found 7 buyers for your data."</p>
    <p style="font-size:12px; font-style:italic; color:#555; margin:0; border-left:3px solid #c8d400; padding-left:10px;">In the arc: the thesis in one experience. The intimacy is real. The extraction is happening at exactly the same moment.</p>
  </div>
</div>
