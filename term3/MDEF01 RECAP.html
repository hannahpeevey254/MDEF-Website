    ★ ★ MDEF Year 1 ★ ★¶

    THE CENTRAL QUESTION

    When does a technological system stop being a tool and become a relationship?

    Everything made this year orbits that question. Not a portfolio of separate experiments — one argument, built piece by piece across two terms, culminating in a live installation at MDEF Fest with 16 real participants and a live surveillance capitalism stock market.

    ★ The Research Arc¶¶

    MOCHI
    DATA + BODY + OWNERSHIP

    What does it mean to tend your own data? A cyberdeck companion that grows or shrinks based on self-care inputs. The body as origin point.

    FIND YOUR MATCH
    DATA AS COMMODITY ← YOU ARE HERE

    The moment data leaves the body and enters the market. 16 participants. 7 buyers. One envelope with a price and a match.

    SAFE HANDS
    DATA AFTER DEATH

    What happens to your data body when you die? Who inherits it? Who can delete it? Who profits? A design problem, not just a legal one.

    ★ The Thesis Position¶¶

    WHAT THE LITERATURE GIVES YOU — AND WHERE IT RUNS OUT

    ZUBOFF — Surveillance Capitalism

    Human experience as free raw material for behavioral data. Frames vulnerability as something imposed on you.

    Gap: doesn't occupy deliberate, authored vulnerability as a design position.

    HAYLES — How We Became Posthuman

    Identity as a pattern of information expressed through the body, not contained by it. The body is not a limitation — it's the theoretical commitment.

    Gap: doesn't extend to the designer's ability to work with that threshold.

    LUPTON — The Quantified Self

    Knowing capitalism: users voluntarily enacting the values of systems extracting from them. Vulnerability framed as risk.

    Gap: doesn't name the space between exposure and protection.

    SAMPAIO — Data Visualization as Portraiture

    Data visualization as subjective expression, not just quantification. Gestures at the tension between exposure and protection.

    Gap: doesn't make that tension a design material.

    THE GAP — WHERE THIS WORK LIVES

    Not total exposure (Poisonous Antidote). Not total protection (privacy by design).
    Conscious calibration of self-exposure — the act of deciding what to render, how, and for whom — as a design act. That's where consent becomes something a designer can work with rather than just a legal checkbox.

    ★ The Projects¶¶

    TERM 1 · DESIGN DIALOGUES
    KINETIC VEIN
    3D print · NeoPixel · screen time data

    A 3D printed Voronoi arm cuff paired with a NeoPixel IV drip installation. Screen time input changes LED state — slow blue breathing at low consumption, frantic red strobe at high. Screen time becomes something that flows through your veins, that the body processes, that has a physiological cost.

    In the arc: makes bodily data externally legible. It doesn't extract silently — it shows you.

    TERM 1 · DESIGN STUDIO 1
    COLLECTIVE HANNAH
    Ollama · Anti-Gravity · 6 AI personas

    Six distinct AI personas constructed from different slices of personal data — location history, sleep data, biometrics, messages, search history, calendar. Each persona represents a different facet of identity as constructed by a machine from a data fragment. The six personas don't agree. They contradict each other. There is no unified self — only the sum of what different systems have decided you mean.

    In the arc: the fragmentation project. Identity is not a stable thing the self holds — it's a pattern that can be assembled, multiplied, and contested.

    TERM 1 · DESIGN STUDIO 1
    MARKET OF ME
    JavaScript · Supabase · streets of Barcelona

    A speculative biometric data stock market deployed as a public action. Personal biometric data — heartrate, steps, stress proxies — treated as a tradeable commodity with fluctuating values. Deployed on the streets of Barcelona, confronting passersby with what their body data is worth and to whom.

    In the arc: the confrontation project. Takes the abstract logic of surveillance capitalism and makes it literal and participatory.

    TERM 2 · COGNITIVE ORGIES I
    AURA
    RPi Pico W · heartbeat sensor · bacterial cellulose

    A wearable, organ-like earpiece that converts heartbeat data into NeoPixel light patterns, encased in bacterial cellulose — a bio-based material grown in the lab. The heartbeat is involuntary, pre-rational, constant. Making it visible is an exposure of something never meant to be read by anyone else. The housing is itself a living material, grown — the biological and the digital in conversation, not opposition.

    In the arc: the most intimate scale. Operates at the level of the body's own signals.

    TERM 2 · COGNITIVE ORGIES II
    ECHO OF MIND
    RPi 5 · Whisper · GPT agent · FLUX.1 · GitHub archive

    A standalone physical device. You speak → Whisper transcribes → a GPT agent processes → FLUX.1 renders as image → result archived to public GitHub. A persistent visual record of thought — externalized, AI-mediated, domestic, present. The act of speaking to it becomes a kind of authorship. The archive fills over time, connecting strangers through their interior lives.

    In the arc: where identity is actively constructed through the pipeline — not just sensed or extracted, but generated.

    TERM 2 · COGNITIVE ORGIES III
    MOCHI
    ESP32-S3 · OLED · pixel art · open source

    A self-contained cyberdeck companion — no cloud, no app store, no extraction. Feed it data through physical buttons: sleep, water, food. It responds with its body. Tend to it and it grows. Neglect it and it shrinks. Abandon it and it dies. Not a wellness tracker. Not a fitness app. A mirror with a body — a physical object that makes the invisible exchange between you and your data visible, chosen, and consequential.

    In the arc: the first act of a trilogy. Here, you choose to tend it. In Find Your Match, it gets extracted. In Safe Hands, it outlives you.

    TERM 3 · MDEF FEST · 18 JUNE 2026
    FIND YOUR MATCH AT ELISAVA
    Next.js · Supabase · Tally · Resend · 16 participants

    QR codes. A dating form. 15 scored questions secretly mapped to 7 real institutional data buyers. A live candlestick stock market. 16 participants. Each finds a folder with their anonymous ID and a euro value — the price their data would fetch on the open market. Inside: a receipt itemizing exactly which answers triggered which buyer. And a match card. A real person. Computed by an algorithm that weighted the filler questions — the ones data markets can't monetize — at 3×.

    "You came looking for a date. We found 7 buyers for your data."

    In the arc: the thesis in one experience. The intimacy is real. The extraction is happening at exactly the same moment.

    ONGOING
    SAFE HANDS
    Supabase · Vercel · Loveable

    A digital legacy platform for managing and transmitting personal digital identity after death. Who inherits your data? Who can delete it? Who profits from it? These are design problems, not just legal ones.

    In the arc: the final-scale project. What happens to the identity you built in every other project when you are no longer there to curate it.

    ★ What’s Next¶¶

    THEORETICAL TASK

    Move from applying existing theory toward contributing your own. The gap is named. Year 2 builds the argument that lives in it — writing theory, not just working in conversation with others'.

    CAPSTONE DIRECTIONS

    An interactive experience placing users in real-time control of their own data exposure. Or a deeper Safe Hands investigation into digital legacy. Or a durational participatory study.

    PROFESSIONAL THREAD

    Studio outreach to Col·lectiu Axolot and Gracias Grecia — spatial intuition, thresholds, choreography of attention as one design intelligence applied at different scales.
