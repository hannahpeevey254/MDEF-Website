    MDEF FEST

    xx# ★ ★ MDEF Fest ★ ★

    ★ Abstract¶¶

    Find Your Match at Elisava.

    Participants encounter QR codes around campus and are drawn in by the oldest social instinct: the desire to connect. They complete what feels like a personality-based dating form, answering 15 questions about their health, lifestyle, emotional patterns, political behaviour, and relationship style. What they are not told is that every answer is secretly mapped to one of seven real institutional data buyer categories and scored against documented market rates for personal data. At the reveal event, each participant finds a folder bearing their anonymous ID and a euro value: the price their data would fetch on the open market.

    “You came looking for a date. We found 7 buyers for your data.”

    MDEF Fest · 18 June 2026 · Room 314, Elisava · 14:30 – 16:30

    RESEARCH ARC
    Mochi  (data + body)  →  Find Your Match  (data as commodity)  →  Safe Hands  (data after death)

    ★ Concept & Research¶¶

    From Biometric Stock Market to Find Your Match.

    This project is the middle act of a three-part research arc. Mochi (2024) explored data, the body, and ownership. Safe Hands will examine data as post-mortem inheritance. Find Your Match sits at the moment of extraction — when personal data leaves the body and enters the market.

    The direct predecessor is the Biometric Stock Market (2024/25), which mapped six biometric data types to four institutional buyers and traced seven feedback loops that compound into a complete digital twin. The key finding: data types are not equal. Health and behavioral intimacy commands the highest market rates. Basic demographics sit at the bottom. This hierarchy became the valuation engine for Find Your Match.

    The Conceptual Trap.

    The shift from the Biometric Stock Market to this project is a shift in stakes. In the earlier piece, the shock came from seeing friends’ data commodified. In this version, the shock is personal: the participant IS the data point. They did not know they signed up for this. They thought they were looking for love. The gap between what they came for and what they found is the work.

    4 BASELINE QUESTIONS

    Name, gender identity, sexuality, course of study. For matching and ID only. Never appear on the stock market.

    15 SCORED QUESTIONS

    Each has 7 answer options, one per institutional buyer. The question is neutral. The data signal lives entirely in the answer chosen.

    4 FILLER QUESTIONS

    Unscored. Distributed between scored questions to maintain the dating app disguise. Feed the matching algorithm at 3× weighting.

    1 MULTI-SELECT

    Q9 on substance use: up to 3 answers, each routing to a different buyer. Behaviour here is not binary.

    The Conceptual Inversion.

    The filler questions are weighted 3× in the matching algorithm — not because they are more useful to buyers, but because they are completely useless to buyers. The questions that data markets cannot monetize are the ones that matter most for human connection. This asymmetry is a conceptual statement built into the code.

    ★ System Pipeline¶¶

    How the technical system works — from QR code to live market.

    PIPELINE
    COLLECTION

    QR Code
    Around Elisava campus. Single line: "Find your match at Elisava." No branding.

    Tally Form
    23 questions. Disguised as dating app. 15 scored, 4 filler, 4 baseline. Webhook fires on submit.

    Buried T&C
    "We may sell your data." In legal font. They agreed. They did not read it.

    BACKEND + DATABASE

    Webhook Receiver
    /api/webhook/route.js on Vercel. Catches Tally POST, verifies signing secret.

    Scoring Engine
    scoringConfig.js: every answer mapped to a buyer. Multipliers applied. Total euros computed.

    Anonymous ID
    5-digit market ID generated per participant.

    Supabase (Postgres)
    4 tables: participants, responses, scores, matching_data.

    MATCHING + DISPLAY

    Admin Page
    /admin/matching. Tiered algorithm: Tier 1 filler @ 3×, Tier 2 @ 2×, Tier 3 @ 1×.

    Email (Resend)
    Staggered 5-min slots. Ominous tone. Confirms time. Reveals nothing.

    Stock Market /market
    Candlestick visualization. Live Supabase subscription. Color by dominant buyer.

    Receipt PDF
    Python script. Connects to Supabase. 16 PDFs for physical folders.

    TECH STACK  ·  Next.js (App Router)  ·  Supabase (Postgres + realtime)  ·  Vercel  ·  Tally (webhook)  ·  Resend (email)  ·  Python (receipts)  ·  GitHub

    ★ The Data Receipt¶¶

    What each participant received — making the invisible translation explicit.

    WHAT THE RECEIPT REVEALS

    SUBJECT ID

    Each participant receives an anonymous 5-digit code (MATCH-0042). This is what appears on the stock market screen — they must find themselves.

    PER-ANSWER BREAKDOWN

    Every one of the 15 scored questions is listed with the exact answer given, the buyer it triggered, and the euro value earned. The question about anger reveals a Pharma signal. The question about protest attendance triggers Government.

    BUYER SUBTOTALS

    The receipt totals each buyer column separately, showing not just what the data is worth but who wants it most and why.

    TOTAL MARKET VALUE

    The sum of all buyer values. This is the number displayed on the live candlestick market screen.

    THE MATCH

    The name and ID of the person they were matched with — computed by the algorithm, weighted toward the filler questions that buyers cannot monetize.

    "The receipt makes the normally invisible translation explicit — this is what you said, and this is what was heard — and the gap between those two things is exactly the hallucination that surveillance capitalism runs on. The system does not know you. It knows what it has decided you mean."

    THE PHYSICAL FOLDER

    EXTERIOR

    Anonymous ID (MATCH-XXXX) and total euro value only. Cold and administrative. No name visible.

    QUESTION DESIGN KEY

    A document explaining what each of the 15 scored questions was actually testing and which buyer category it fed.

    RECEIPT

    Full per-question buyer breakdown. 15 questions, 15 scores, 7 buyer subtotals, total market value.

    MATCH CARD

    The name of their matched person — computed, real, the counterweight to the number on the screen.

    ★ The Reveal¶¶

    MDEF Fest · 18 June 2026 · Room 314 · 14:30 – 16:30 · 16 real participants

    01 ARRIVE

    Staggered 5-minute email slots. Trickle in rather than arrive as a group. Low-level unease already building.

    02 FIND YOUR FOLDER

    Table with physical folders. Exterior: MATCH-XXXX and a euro value. Cold. Administrative. No explanation.

    03 OPEN IT

    Inside: a receipt itemizing every answer and its buyer score. A question design key. A match card with a real name.

    04 LOOK UP

    Live candlestick market on screen. Find your 5-digit ID. See your value. See your dominant buyer category.

    05 READ THE T&C

    Printed scroll below the screen. Full legal text. Somewhere in the middle: the clause that told them this would happen.

    06 FIND YOUR MATCH

    That person is somewhere in the room. The match is real, computed, the counterweight to the market price.

    THREE INTERLOCKING INTENTIONS

    EXPERIENTIAL

    Most people understand data surveillance intellectually but have never felt it. The installation creates the feeling — specifically the retroactive feeling of having consented to something you did not understand.

    STRUCTURAL

    By building the valuation logic from real research into what data types are worth to real institutional buyers, the market prices on screen are not arbitrary. They reflect the genuine hierarchy of surveillance capitalism.

    RELATIONAL

    The project uses the most human desire — to find a connection — as the vector for the most dehumanizing system — to be quantified as a tradeable asset. The match is real. The number is real. Both are true simultaneously.

    ★ Database Architecture¶¶

    Supabase (Postgres) · 4 tables · real-time subscriptions · service role key server-side only

    SCHEMA

    participants

    id text PK — 5-digit anonymous market ID

    name, email, instagram, gender_identity, sexuality, course

    match_id, arrival_slot, is_synthetic, created_at


    responses

    id uuid PK · participant_id FK

    question_id (Q1–Q15) · answer · buyer_awarded · created_at

    scores

    participant_id FK (unique)

    pharma_points, govt_points, bigtech_points...

    pharma_euros, govt_euros... total_euros · dominant_buyer


    matching_data

    participant_id FK

    q17–q20 filler answers (first date self, love language,

    most yourself when, can tell about someone by)

    CASCADE DELETE ORDER: matching_data → scores → responses → participants
